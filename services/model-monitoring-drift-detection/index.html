Know When Your AI Model Starts Getting It Wrong

AI models degrade silently in production. Our monitoring service detects drift before it becomes a business problem - so you retrain before accuracy drops.

The Challenge

You might be experiencing...

Your fraud detection model was 91% accurate at launch six months ago but you have no visibility into whether that accuracy is still holding.
Market conditions in UAE changed significantly and you suspect your demand forecasting model is now making poor predictions - but you have no monitoring in place to confirm.
A data source upstream of your model changed its schema silently and you only discovered the degradation when a business analyst noticed the predictions looked wrong.
Regulators are asking you to demonstrate ongoing monitoring of AI model performance for CBUAE or DHA compliance - and you have no monitoring infrastructure.

Model monitoring is the operational foundation of production AI. A model trained today on excellent data will drift as markets shift, customer behaviour evolves, and the world changes around it. Without monitoring, you only discover degradation when a business analyst notices wrong-looking outputs - months after accuracy started declining.

Common Questions

Frequently Asked Questions

What is model drift and why does it happen?

Model drift occurs when the statistical relationship between your input features and the target you are predicting changes over time. Data drift means the distribution of your input features has shifted - customers are behaving differently, transactions look different, or property market characteristics have changed. Concept drift means the relationship between features and outcome has changed - patterns that predicted fraud in 2023 no longer match fraud in 2025. Both types cause model accuracy to degrade. In UAE markets with rapid economic changes (real estate cycles, regulatory changes, demographic shifts), drift happens faster than in stable Western markets.

How often should models be retrained?

It depends on the rate of drift in your domain. Fraud detection models in UAE fintech may need retraining monthly due to evolving fraud patterns. Property valuation models may need retraining quarterly as market cycles shift. Demand forecasting models in retail may need retraining weekly ahead of seasonal events (Ramadan, DSF, back-to-school). Our monitoring service measures your actual drift rate and recommends retraining frequency based on observed data, not a fixed schedule.

What is the difference between monitoring and logging?

Logging records what happened: every prediction, with input features and output score, timestamped. Monitoring analyses the log to detect patterns: are prediction scores shifting? Are input feature distributions diverging from baseline? Is the model accuracy (where ground truth is available) declining? You need both - logging without monitoring gives you a database you never look at; monitoring without logging gives you alerts with no root cause data. We set up both.

How does model monitoring satisfy UAE regulatory requirements?

CBUAE AI Guidelines (2023) and DHA Digital Health Standards require financial institutions and healthcare providers to demonstrate ongoing monitoring of AI system performance. Our monitoring service produces the audit trail regulators expect: timestamped performance records, drift event logs, alert histories, and monthly review reports. For CBUAE compliance, we align monitoring documentation to the accountability and transparency requirements of the CBUAE AI framework.
